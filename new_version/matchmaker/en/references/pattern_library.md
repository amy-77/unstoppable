# Conflict Pattern Library

## Wrong Timing to Enter the Market (New)

**Pattern Definition**: The seeker's current state is unsuitable for entering the dating market (psychological damage unrepaired / core assets not yet accumulated / structural disadvantages too heavy). Forcing entry is itself the core conflict. The system should recognize that "pause entry, focus on self-improvement" is the optimal strategy, rather than defaulting to a market-matching solution.

**Trigger Conditions**:

- Structural disadvantages stacked too heavily (multiple hard disqualifiers coexist), making entry ROI extremely low
- Psychological trauma unhealed (severe damage from previous relationship unrepaired), entry will inevitably repeat old patterns
- Core assets have not yet accumulated to the entry threshold (income/education/physique etc. have hard disqualifiers that could be improved in the short term)

**Strategy Direction**: Pause entry + targeted self-improvement (fold your hand rather than play it)

**Typical Cases**:

- **AY104** (stacked structural disadvantages): Multiple hard disqualifiers coexist; should first concentrate resources on targeted improvement, then re-enter the market once core weaknesses are addressed
  -> Pause entry, develop a 6–12 month improvement plan, prioritize fixing the weakness with the highest ROI.
- Unhealed psychological trauma case: Previous relationship caused deep psychological damage; entering the market in this state will only attract the same type of partner or repeat old patterns
  -> Complete psychological repair first (professional counseling / self-healing), establish healthy relationship cognition before re-entering.

## Lacking "Lover" Qualities (New)

**Pattern Definition**: Distinct from "low emotional value output" (skewing male, skewing insufficient delivery capacity), this pattern focuses on "lack of interactive attraction" — the seeker has excellent specs but cannot create emotional tension, cannot flirt playfully, cannot proactively advance a relationship, and lacks lover-type qualities. The core problem is not channels or standards, but emotional interaction ability itself.

**Trigger Conditions**:

- Excellent specs (appearance/education/family background all solid) but chronically unable to enter intimate relationships
- Normal social skills but lacking opposite-sex attraction / emotional tension
- Evaluated as "nice person" or "impressive" but never treated as a romantic prospect

**Strategy Direction**: Emotional interaction skill training (not channel adjustment / standard management)

**Typical Cases**:

- **AY165** (sheltered high-spec passive woman): Excellent across all dimensions but cannot flirt, cannot create ambiguity, cannot proactively advance — causing the opposite sex to see her only as a "good girl" rather than "a girl I want to pursue"
  -> Core training: Learn to emit feminine attraction signals, create emotional tension, proactively create opportunities for intimate interaction. The problem is not on the market side, but in activating her own lover qualities.

## Low-Score Case Anchor Summary (case_digest supplement)

The following cases are historically lowest-scored cases. Their correct attribution directions are recorded here to prevent repeating similar errors:

- **AY104**: Core conflict should be attributed to "stacked structural disadvantages making market entry inadvisable." Correct strategy tempo is "fold and improve" rather than "play and match." Common system misjudgment: defaulting to channel/matching solutions while ignoring the necessity of pausing entry.
- **AY144**: Core conflict should be attributed to "incorrect game posture in an asymmetric value situation." When the seeker has already locked onto a target far exceeding their own conditions, strategy should switch from "equal screening" to "lock the resource, lower posture, go all-in on binding." Common system misjudgment: still giving screening advice based on equal-game logic.
- **AY165**: Core conflict should be attributed to "lacking lover qualities / emotional interaction ability" (internal personality weakness), not market structure problems. Correct strategy is emotional capability training (learn to flirt, create tension), not channel adjustment or standard relaxation. Common system misjudgment: attributing to external market problems.
- **AY178**: Core conflict should be attributed to "lack of emotional sensitivity" rather than surface labels (e.g., divorced). The real weakness of high-material-control men is insufficient emotional supply capability; strategy should focus on internal personality transformation. Common system misjudgment: attributing the problem to market discrimination against the divorced label.

**Attribution Priority Principle**: When a case simultaneously presents external market structure problems and internal personality weaknesses, prioritize attributing to internal personality weaknesses (changeable, higher ROI), then address external structural issues.

**Strategy Tempo Judgment Principle**:
- Play the hand (offensive / market-adaptive): applicable when core assets are in place, psychological state is healthy, and market positioning is clear
- Fold the hand (defensive / self-transformation / pause): applicable when structural disadvantages are too heavy, psychology is unrepaired, or core capabilities have not yet accumulated
